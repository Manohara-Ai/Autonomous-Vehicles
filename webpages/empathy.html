Questions for Autonomous Technology Experts:

    What aspect of AV are you currently working on, and why is it of importance?

    What recent advancements in AI and sensor technology have had the most impact on AVs?

    What are the difficulties you are encountering in your research?

    What are the main safety challenges?

    How do you see government policies evolving to shape the future of AVs?

    Do you see AVs as a far-fetched dream, or are they feasible in the near future in India?

Answers from Autonomous Technology Experts:

    We are focusing on improving real-time object detection as it is critical for AV safety and efficiency.

    Recent advancements in LIDAR technology and AI-driven predictive modeling have been game changers.

    The major challenges include data integration, high computational demands, and unpredictable real-world scenarios.

    Ensuring fail-safe systems and reducing reliance on connectivity in critical situations remain key safety challenges.

    Government policies are gradually adapting, with a focus on safety standards and creating test environments for AVs.

    AVs are a realistic possibility in India, provided infrastructure upgrades and regulatory support are prioritized.

Questions We Asked Teachers:

    What is your understanding of autonomous vehicles (AVs) and their potential impact on education?

    How do you think AV technology could influence the daily commute for students?

    What concerns do you have about AVs regarding safety or reliability?

    How can AVs improve accessibility for students with disabilities?

    What role should schools play in educating students about AV technology?

Answers from Teachers:

    Autonomous vehicles could revolutionize commutes, but many are unsure about how soon the technology will mature.

    Teachers believe AVs could make school transportation safer, reducing human errors by drivers.

    Safety and cost are the primary concerns expressed, particularly in areas with inconsistent infrastructure.

    AVs have the potential to provide better mobility options for students with disabilities, offering a more inclusive experience.

    Teachers suggest schools should incorporate AV technology topics into science and technology curriculums to prepare students for the future.

Questions We Asked Drivers:

    What is your understanding of autonomous vehicles (AVs) and how do you feel about them?

    Do you think AVs could improve road safety? Why or why not?

    What are your biggest concerns regarding AVs?

    How would you feel about sharing the road with AVs?

    Do you think AVs will impact your job as a driver? If so, how?

    How do you see the transition from traditional to autonomous vehicles happening in India?

Answers from Drivers:

    Many drivers expressed curiosity about AVs but admitted to a lack of detailed understanding of the technology.

    Some believe AVs could improve road safety by reducing human errors, while others worry about their ability to handle complex traffic situations.

    The primary concerns include job security, reliability of the technology, and the cost of implementing AVs.

    Most drivers are hesitant about sharing the road with AVs due to trust issues and unpredictable interactions.

    Many fear job displacement but are open to learning new skills if opportunities arise in the AV ecosystem.

    Drivers think the transition to AVs in India will be slow due to infrastructure challenges and high costs.
